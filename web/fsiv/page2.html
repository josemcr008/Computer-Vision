Machine Learning

Is the subfield of computer science that, according to Arthur Samuel in 1959, gives "computers the ability to learn without being explicity progammed". Classification is a general process

Classification: Ingredients

  • Dataset: contains samples and labels
  • Splits:
    • Training: to learn parameters of the model (classifier)
    • Validation: to tune hyperparameters, eg K in kNN
    • Test: to predict performance at deployment time

Classification: Metrics

  • Accuracy: how often the classifier makes the correct prediction.
    Acc = (#correct / #total) * 100
  • Confusion matrix: shows details about correct and incorrect Classification for each category

Types of classifiers

  • Supervised: labels to every sample
  • Semi-Supervised: labels to samples but not everyone
  • Unsupervised: no labels

SVM Support Vector Machines

Margin -> parameter. The separation between the two clases.

Multiclass

From binary classifier to multiclass

  • One vs All : Take the arg max
    • f1(x): C1 vs {C2,C3}
    • f2(x): C2 vs {C1,C3}
    • f3(x): C3 vs {C2,C1}
  • One vs One : Take the arg max
    • f1(x): C1 vs C2
    • f2(x): C1 vs C3
    • f3(x): C2 vs C3
