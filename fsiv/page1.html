Optical flow

A pattern of apparent motion of the object. We use it to obtain objects that are moving between two images. OF is a vector. Three assumptions

  • Brightness does not change from frame to frame
  • Small movements
  • Neighboring points belong to same surface and have similar motion
I(x(t), t) = I (x(t+dt), t+dt)

I= intensity; x=spacial position; t=time; dt= increment of time


Two points: (2,10), (4,12). The optical flow will be: (2,10) - (4-12) = (2,2) = (u,v)

Segmentation based on optical flow

Optical flow is used to represent movement. Is used to blur the background of an image and we can use it to read, for example, the movement of a person. Basic methods:

  1. Compute the optical flow between sequential frames: t-1 with 1
  2. For each pixel, compute magnitude of optical flow
  3. Normalize OF magnitude in [0,1]
  4. If Mag(y,x) > T, then "(y,x) is foreground"
